Trends, Networks, and Critical Thinking in the 21st Century

A specialized subject for Humanities and Social
Sciences strand during the first semester in 12th Grade.

Introduction

This subject gives students the chance to identify patterns in developing trends and deduce their implications. It helps them refine their analytical and ability to think creatively which is crucial for making decisions and comprehending the ethics of care. A good set of criteria is used to analyze and accept or reject 21st-century global trends. Students will be required to develop and evaluate situations that will test their ability to (1) establish opinions about problems or concerns, (2) suggest solutions, and (3) imagine alternate futures. Lastly, the linkages between the neurological connections and social reality will also become clear to the students.
