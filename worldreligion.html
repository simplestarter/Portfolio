Introduction to World Religions and Belief Systems

A specialized subject for Humanities and Social
Sciences strand during the first semester in 11th Grade.

Introduction

The subject explores the main tenets and practices of major world religions: Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism, Theravada Buddhism, Mahayana Buddhism, Confucianism, Taoism and Shintoism. It aims to help learners understand the historical contexts of various religions, appreciate their uniqueness and similarities and promote mutual understanding among believers of different faiths. In this subject, students are expected to demonstrate understanding and appreciation of one’s faith and that of others.

Reflection

Religion is the most complicated issue that we, humans, come across while exisiting in this diverse world. There are countless never-ending arguments and discussions that tackle different matters of religion. They are firmly fighting for what belief they think is right. People can only agree to disagree. Nevertheless, one thing is for sure: the religion of a person will never give us an excuse to discrimniate them nor justify any prejudices. Therefore, mutual respect and tolerance must be always present especially if we want to live in harmony.
