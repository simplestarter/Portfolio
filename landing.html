Discipline and Ideas in Social Sciences

A specialized subject for Humanities and Social
Sciences strand during the first semester in 11th Grade.

Introduction

Students are introduced to the fundamental ideas, topics, and analytical approaches in the areas that compose the social sciences in this subject. The discussion of prominent figures and concepts in these fields is followed by an application of these principles to the social science general framework and contemporary global developments.

Structural-Functionalism

In this activity, we were given five different words whichi we have to describe or “I-flex mo lang” their main functions in the society. We then write our answer on the given blanks. Then be able to answer the question at the center.

Structural-Functionalism

After gaining broader insights about Sturctural functionalism, our teacher then tasked us to answer the given “GRAPHIC ORGANIZER” where we were able to write our thoughts and ideas on the blanks.

Marxism

As for this activity, our teacher provided some daily-life scenario depicting social conflicts. Then we analyze each situation and think of possible ways to resolving such struggles. And wrote our answers for the query "How can you be part of the solution or change?" on the space allotted.

Reflection

Our life is full of interaction; one cannot survive without having any social relations with other people. With this subject, my concept about the humanity as a whole become broader. The realization that we, humans, have numerous principles that are significant on a daily basis have now fostered on my mind. And this will guide me how to act properly as I grow older and encounter different individuals in my life.
