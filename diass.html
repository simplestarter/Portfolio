Discipline and Ideas in Applied Social Sciences

A specialized subject for Humanities and Social
Sciences strand during the second semester in 11th Grade.

Introduction


The Applied Social Sciences such as Counseling, Social Work, and Communication, which have their roots in the aspect of Humanities, are introduced in this subject. This broaden the knowledge of students on psychological, sociological, anthropological, and other social science ideas and principles. The subject focuses on the procedures and applications of social science discipline in crucial development sectors while highlighting the seamless interconnectedness of the social science branches.
At the conclusion of this discipline, students will be able to: demonstrate competence in interacting and relating with other people, groups, and communities; apply social science principles, practices, and tools in addressing the development areas identified by the class; and analyze how these applied disciplines' processes function in particular life situations.

But the job is also important. Now, what will be the decision? We then explain each choice: "Will quit the job." and "Will stay on the job." and supported our stance by answering these follow-up questions: "Why? Which is more ethical? Why is it more ethical?"

Reflection

As an individual who is part of a society, social interaction on a daily basis will always be present in our life. As we immersed in the content of this subject, I learned the significance that society brings to ourselves. Although society is too complex to understand, it should still be our utmost priority to be aware about its concept that it possess for us to properly take part in it. We are part of this society and the social interactions that we will create will be the sole determinant whether we will exist in a healthy or toxic surroundings.
